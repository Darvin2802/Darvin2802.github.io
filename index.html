What I Built

A 10-step mandatory research workflow that encodes senior analyst judgment into a reusable LLM system — covering transaction identification, document retrieval, ownership mapping, deal mechanics extraction, advisor identification, post-announcement monitoring, and a complete source register. Operates on a deal headline and produces a fixed 12-section professional memo every time.

Key Design Decisions

  • Controlled vocabulary of 12 transaction types for consistent classification
  • Mandatory ±7 day presentation search window around announcement date — catches investor decks manual research misses
  • Document priority hierarchy: press release → exchange filing → SEC → presentations → merger agreement → media
  • 15 anti-hallucination hard rules — prohibits "likely," "appears to be," "may have" unless quoted from source
  • Ownership chain mapping: Entity → immediate parent → intermediate parent(s) → ultimate parent, stops explicitly where confirmation ends
  • Legal term simplification — complex clauses quoted then explained in plain business English
  • Full source register on every output: title, type, party, date, URL, access status, source quality
  • Failsafe rule — stops and asks for clarification rather than proceeding with a potentially wrong deal

What Makes This Different

The previous three workflows automate repetitive high-volume tasks. This one encodes expert judgment. The 10-step sequence, document hierarchy, ownership rules, anti-hallucination guardrails, and failsafe rule come from knowing what experienced M&A analysts check, in what order, and why. Encoding that into a reusable system is a fundamentally different kind of work — it raises the quality floor so a junior analyst using this system produces a more complete, better-sourced memo than a senior analyst working manually.

The 10-Step Mandatory Workflow

STEP 1
Identify & Verify Transaction
Parse input headline. Verify from primary sources. Classify using controlled 12-type vocabulary. Confirm all parties, dates, stake, and geography before proceeding.
STEP 2
Full Document Retrieval
Search 8 source categories: buyer, seller, target, exchange/regulator, advisor, media, archived pages, local-language sources. ±7 day presentation window. Priority hierarchy enforced.
STEP 3
Transaction Snapshot
Compact verified summary: deal type, announcement/signing/closing dates, status, buyers, sellers, target, stake, geography, deal value, consideration mix.
STEP 4
Ownership Structure Mapping
Trace buyer and seller ownership chains as of announcement date. Entity → immediate parent → intermediate → ultimate parent. Reports only confirmed links. States explicitly where confirmation stops.
STEP 5
Transaction Details Extraction
Deal value breakdown: cash, equity, debt assumed, working capital adjustment, earnout, EV, equity value. Plus conditions precedent, regulatory approvals, financing, termination rights, break fees.
STEP 6
Asset / Company Detail
Separated into A) confirmed from transaction documents and B) supplemental from other sources. Covers business description, capacity, contracts, financials, historical ownership, strategic rationale.
STEP 7
Advisor Identification
Financial, legal, fairness opinion, lender/arranger, PR, technical, proxy, accounting advisors. Organized by side. Searches advisor press releases, law firm matter pages, tombstones if not in primary docs.
STEPS 8–10
Post-Announcement + Source Register
Monitors closing, amendment, or termination. Consolidated "Not Found" section for full transparency. Complete source register: every source with title, type, party, URL, access status, quality rating.
